Google clamps down on Antigravity 'malicious usage', cutting off OpenClaw users in sweeping ToS enforcement move

VentureBeat
Original Source
VentureBeat
Read Original Article
// Analysis

Google has restricted access to its Antigravity "vibe coding" platform for certain users, specifically those integrating it with the open-source autonomous AI agent OpenClaw, citing "malicious usage." This enforcement action, which took place over the weekend and on February 23rd, resulted in some users reporting a loss of access to their Google accounts. Google stated that these users were leveraging Antigravity to access a larger volume of Gemini tokens via third-party platforms like OpenClaw, which led to service degradation for other Antigravity customers.

// Why It Matters
Signal Intelligence Assessment
  • OpenClaw developers must now re-evaluate their integration strategies and dependencies, as Google's restrictions and OpenClaw's subsequent removal of Google support introduce direct infrastructure uncertainty for OpenClaw-based workflows.
  • This incident signals a critical shift in the agentic AI ecosystem towards "walled garden" platforms, compelling practitioners to anticipate reduced interoperability and prioritize proprietary integrations over open-source solutions for frontier models.
  • Google's enforcement, following Anthropic's similar actions, establishes a precedent that providers will prioritize system load and revenue capture, shifting how builders should approach rate limits and Terms of Service adherence in agent deployments.
  • The friction between Google and an OpenAI-adjacent project highlights the escalating competitive landscape between major AI platform providers, indicating potential future incompatibilities and strategic divergences for developers navigating the ecosystem.
